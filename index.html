Key Production Elements

Intro: Glockenspiel plays descending 4-note motif (D–C–Bb–A), fingerpicked acoustic guitar, synth pad fades in slowly, faint ambient night sounds (crickets, soft wind), small room reverb (0.8s).

Verse 1: Upright bass fingerpicked dry, kick drum on beats 1 and 3 only with felt-mallet style, close intimate mix, no electric instruments.

Chorus: Full band enters together, grand piano replaces Rhodes, wide stereo spread with hall reverb (2.2s) and 20ms pre-delay on vocals, strings swell upward on resolution.

Bridge: Drops to solo cello for stark contrast, reversed guitar texture for dreamlike feel, plate reverb (3.0s) creates unmoored searching, final piano + string hit acts as emotional thunderclap before silence.

Final Chorus: Key shifts to D major, full orchestral swell, 2–3 dB louder via arrangement (not compression), BPM nudges to 74 BPM for emotional lift, hall reverb (2.5s) wider than Chorus 2.

Outro: Everything falls away except piano and glockenspiel, glockenspiel repeats intro motif, cathedral reverb (4.5s) lets notes dissolve, resolves to tonic D.
