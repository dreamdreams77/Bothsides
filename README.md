# Both Sides of the Night

**Original Song | Written and Produced by Laura Johnston**

---

## Overview

An intimate exploration of love across distance and time zones. *Both Sides of the Night* examines the vulnerability of reaching across 17 hours, the patterns of emotional spiral and recovery, and what it means to choose someone every single day despite the weight of geography.

**Duration:** 4:10 – 4:30  
**Key:** D minor → D major (Final Chorus)  
**Tempo:** 72 BPM (flex to 74 BPM at Final Chorus)

### Key Instrumentation

**Intro:** Glockenspiel (descending D–C–Bb–A motif), fingerpicked acoustic guitar, synth pad, ambient night sounds

**Verse 1:** Upright bass fingerpicked, kick drum (beats 1 & 3), dry intimate mix

**Chorus:** Grand piano, string quartet, wide stereo spread, hall reverb (2.2s)

**Bridge:** Solo cello, reversed guitar texture, plate reverb (3.0s)

**Final Chorus:** Key shift to D major, full orchestral swell, cathedral-scale reverb

### Reverb Map

- **Intro/Verse:** Small room (0.8s)
- **Chorus:** Hall + 20ms pre-delay (2.2s)
- **Bridge:** Long plate (3.0s)
- **Final Chorus:** Hall (2.5s)
- **Outro:** Cathedral (4.5s)

### Mastering Targets

- **LUFS:** -14 LUFS integrated
- **True Peak:** -1 dBTP
- **Dynamic Range:** DR10 minimum

## About the Song

A deeply personal exploration of distance, vulnerability, and commitment. The song traces the architecture of a long-distance relationship across 17 time zones, documenting the moments where patterns emerge, where spirals occur, and where two people choose each other anyway.
